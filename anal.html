Analogous Colours

Analogous colours are colours that are located next to another on the colour wheel.

Whereas complementary colours will provide contrast, analogous colours compliment each other. Analogous colours are different from complementary and triadic colours in that there is no set amount of colours used in an analogous colour scheme. However, the colours used must be located next to another on the colour wheel.

Analogous colour schemes are harmonious and pleasing to the eye. However, they are less vibrant and contrasting than complementary or triadic colours. This colour scheme tends to stand out less.

Example: Orange, Red-Orange, Red
